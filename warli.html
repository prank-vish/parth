About Warli

Warli painting is a traditional tribal art form of the indigenous Warli people of Maharashtra. It is known for its simple yet striking depictions of daily life and nature using white paint on a red or brown background.

Warli art is characterized by its use of geometric shapes such as circles, triangles, and squares to create scenes of farming, festivals, dances, and wildlife.

This art form has been passed down through generations and continues to be a vital part of the Warli community's cultural identity.

Styles and Techniques

Warli art primarily uses white pigment made from rice paste, and paintings are created on a red ochre or mud base. The simple and expressive forms depict scenes from the Warli's everyday life and their close relationship with nature.

History and Significance

Warli painting has its roots in the ancient tribal traditions of the Warli people. It has gained recognition and appreciation both in India and internationally for its unique style and cultural significance.
