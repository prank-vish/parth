About Dahi Handi

Dahi Handi, also known as Janmashtami, celebrates the birth of Lord Krishna. The festival is marked by the playful ritual of breaking a clay pot filled with curd, suspended high above the ground, by forming human pyramids.

The celebration is particularly popular in Maharashtra, where teams compete to break the handi, and is accompanied by devotional songs, dances, and festive foods.

Significance

Dahi Handi signifies the playful and mischievous nature of Lord Krishna, who is said to have stolen butter and curd from houses in his childhood. The festival celebrates his birth and his many divine acts, showcasing the joy and devotion of his followers.

Traditions

Traditions include forming human pyramids to break the clay pot filled with curd, singing devotional songs, and preparing special sweets and dishes. The festival is a lively and joyful occasion, reflecting the playful spirit of Lord Krishna.
