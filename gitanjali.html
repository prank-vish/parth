About Gitanjali

Gitanjali, or Song Offerings, is a collection of 103 poems written by the Bengali poet Rabindranath Tagore. It was published in 1910 and won Tagore the Nobel Prize for Literature in 1913.

Overview

Gitanjali's themes include devotion, the journey of life, the nature of the divine, and the interconnectedness of humanity and nature. Tagore's poetic prose reflects a deep spirituality and the search for inner peace and enlightenment.

Significance

Gitanjali is considered a masterpiece of Bengali literature and a cornerstone of Tagore's literary legacy. Its translations have introduced millions of readers worldwide to Tagore's visionary and poetic imagination.
