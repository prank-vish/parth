About Makar Sankranti

Makar Sankranti marks the transition of the Sun into the zodiac sign of Capricorn (Makara) and is celebrated with kite flying, bonfires, and feasting on traditional sweets made from sesame seeds and jaggery.

The festival signifies the end of winter and the arrival of longer days, symbolizing the beginning of a new harvest season.

Significance

Diwali signifies the spiritual victory of light over darkness, good over evil, and knowledge over ignorance. It is celebrated with the lighting of oil lamps, decorating homes, and performing religious rituals.

Traditions

People celebrate Makar Sankranti by flying colorful kites, preparing traditional sweets like tilgul, and lighting bonfires. The festival is marked by community gatherings, feasting, and various cultural events that bring people together.
