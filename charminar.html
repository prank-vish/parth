About Charminar

The Charminar, located in Hyderabad, India, is a historic monument and mosque that stands as a symbol of the city. It was built in 1591 by Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah to commemorate the end of a deadly plague.

The structure is known for its four grand arches, each facing a cardinal direction, and its intricate stucco decorations.

The Charminar is a significant landmark in Hyderabad and a popular tourist destination.

Architecture

The Charminar's architecture is a blend of Indo-Islamic styles, featuring four grand arches, a mosque on its top floor, and 45 prayer spaces. The minarets, which are 56 meters tall, are crowned by bulbous domes and have intricate designs carved into their surfaces.

History

The Charminar was built to commemorate the eradication of a deadly plague that had ravaged the city. It served as a centerpiece of Hyderabad and a landmark from which major thoroughfares diverged. Over the centuries, it has witnessed numerous historical events and continues to be a focal point for the city's cultural and social activities.
