About Red Fort

The Red Fort, located in Delhi, India, is a historic fortification and a symbol of India's rich heritage. It was built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in 1638 as the palace fort of his capital, Shahjahanabad.

The fort is known for its massive enclosing walls of red sandstone, which give it its name. It served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors for nearly 200 years.

This UNESCO World Heritage site is a prominent tourist attraction and a significant landmark in Indian history.

Architecture

The Red Fort showcases Mughal architecture with its red sandstone walls, intricate carvings, and beautiful gardens. The fort complex includes several palaces, audience halls, and mosques, reflecting the grandeur and opulence of the Mughal era. The Diwan-i-Aam (Hall of Public Audience) and the Diwan-i-Khas (Hall of Private Audience) are notable structures within the fort.

History

The Red Fort has witnessed several significant events in Indian history, including the end of the Mughal Empire and the beginning of British colonial rule. It was from the ramparts of this fort that India's first Prime Minister, Jawaharlal Nehru, delivered his famous speech on India's independence on August 15, 1947.
