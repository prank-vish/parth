About Panipuri

Panipuri, also known as golgappa, is a popular street food in India. It consists of hollow, crispy puris filled with spicy, tangy water (pani), tamarind chutney, and a mixture of flavored water.

Ingredients

The key ingredients for Panipuri include semolina (sooji), wheat flour, chickpeas, tamarind, spices like cumin and black salt, and mint leaves. The preparation involves frying puris and assembling them with the flavorful fillings.

History

Panipuri originated in the Indian subcontinent, where it is enjoyed as a popular street snack. Its name varies by region—golgappa in North India, puchka in West Bengal, and gupchup in Odisha—reflecting its widespread popularity and regional adaptations.
