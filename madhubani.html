About Madhubani

Madhubani, also known as Mithila art, is a traditional Indian art form that originated in the Mithila region of Bihar. It is characterized by complex geometrical patterns and vibrant colors.

Madhubani paintings often depict nature, mythology, and religious themes, reflecting the cultural and social values of the community.

This art form is practiced by the women of the region, and it has gained international recognition for its unique style and beauty.

Styles and Techniques

Madhubani art is known for its distinctive styles, including Bharni, Katchni, Tantrik, Godna, and Kohbar. These styles use different techniques and motifs to create stunning pieces of art.

History and Significance

Madhubani paintings date back to the Ramayana period when King Janaka commissioned artists to create paintings for his daughter Sita's wedding. The tradition has continued through generations, preserving the cultural heritage of the region.
