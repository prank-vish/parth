About India Gate

India Gate, located in New Delhi, India, is a war memorial dedicated to the 82,000 soldiers of the Indian Army who died during World War I and the Third Anglo-Afghan War.

Designed by Sir Edwin Lutyens, India Gate stands at 42 meters tall and is reminiscent of the Arc de Triomphe in Paris. The names of the soldiers who died in the wars are inscribed on its walls.

India Gate is an iconic landmark in Delhi and a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors from all over the world.

Architecture

India Gate's architecture is inspired by the triumphal arches of ancient Rome. It is built from red and yellow sandstone and granite, with a shallow domed bowl at the top used for burning oil on special occasions. The structure stands on a low base of red Bharatpur stone and rises in stages to a large molding.

History

India Gate was unveiled by Lord Irwin, the then Viceroy of India, in 1931. It is a symbol of the sacrifices made by Indian soldiers and serves as a reminder of India's colonial past. The Amar Jawan Jyoti, or the Flame of the Immortal Soldier, was added after India's independence and burns day and night under the arch to commemorate the soldiers who died in the Indo-Pakistani War of 1971.
