About Hawa Mahal

Hawa Mahal, located in Jaipur, India, is an exquisite palace known for its unique architecture and historical significance. Built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh, it was designed by Lal Chand Ustad.

The palace is constructed from red and pink sandstone and stands at the edge of the City Palace, extending to the women's chambers or Zenana.

Hawa Mahal is a prominent landmark in Jaipur and attracts visitors from all over the world who come to admire its beauty and historical significance.

Architecture

Hawa Mahal is a five-story pyramidal structure, famous for its 953 small windows, called jharokhas, decorated with intricate latticework. The purpose of the lattice was to allow royal ladies to observe everyday life and festivals celebrated in the street below without being seen, as was the custom of the purdah.

History

The Hawa Mahal was constructed to extend the Zenana, or women's chambers, and provide the royal ladies a place to observe the street festivals while remaining unseen from the outside. The design of the Hawa Mahal also helped keep the palace cool during hot summer months, thanks to its unique structure and the airflow created by its many windows.
