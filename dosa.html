About Dosa

Dosa is a fermented crepe made from rice and black lentils. It is a staple dish in South Indian cuisine and is typically served with coconut chutney and sambar.

Back to Cuisines Try Quiz

Ingredients

The key ingredients for dosa include rice, black lentils, fenugreek seeds, and salt. The batter is fermented overnight to achieve the characteristic taste and texture.

History

Dosa has ancient origins in South India and has evolved into various regional variations. It is known for its crispiness and nutritious value, making it a popular choice for breakfast or dinner.
