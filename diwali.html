About Diwali

Diwali, also known as the Festival of Lights, is one of the most popular Hindu festivals. It symbolizes the victory of light over darkness and good over evil. Celebrations include lighting oil lamps, bursting fireworks, and exchanging sweets and gifts.

The festival is celebrated with great fervor across India, marking the return of Lord Rama to Ayodhya after 14 years of exile, as described in the epic Ramayana.

Significance

Diwali signifies the spiritual victory of light over darkness, good over evil, and knowledge over ignorance. It is celebrated with the lighting of oil lamps, decorating homes, and performing religious rituals.

Traditions

The festival involves various traditions such as cleaning and decorating homes, preparing festive foods, and participating in religious ceremonies. People also exchange gifts and sweets, and light fireworks to celebrate the joyous occasion.
