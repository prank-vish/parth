About Durga Puja

Durga Puja is a major Hindu festival celebrated to honor the goddess Durga. It is particularly popular in West Bengal, Assam, Tripura, Odisha, and Bihar. The festival involves elaborate rituals, worship, and cultural performances.

Durga Puja marks the victory of goddess Durga over the demon Mahishasura, symbolizing the triumph of good over evil. It is celebrated with great joy and enthusiasm, with pandals (temporary structures) hosting intricately crafted idols of Durga, cultural programs, and feasts.

Significance

Durga Puja celebrates the power of goddess Durga, who embodies courage and divine feminine energy. It signifies the victory of good over evil and the removal of obstacles from one's life.

Celebrations

The festival involves several days of rituals, including worship of the goddess, cultural performances like music and dance, and community gatherings. Pandals (decorated tents) are set up to display idols of Durga, often depicting her in various forms and colors.
